The champagne flute, or flûte à Champagne in French, is a stem glass that typically holds 180 to 300 ml (6.1 to 10.1 US fl oz) of liquid. It has either a tall, tapered conical form or an elongated, slender bowl.

Pilsner glasses have a tapering shape, are tall, and are made of thin glass (smaller at the bottom and wider at the top). A pilsner's broad mouth helps to hold the beer's foamy head for a fragrant experience, and its tapering form allows beer bubbles to ascend to the top of the glass.

A pint glass is a type of drinking vessel designed to carry either an American pint, which is 16 US fluid ounces, or a British imperial pint, which is 20 imperial fluid ounces (568 ml) (473 ml).

A glass tumbler with a capacity of 240 to 350 millilitres is known as a highball glass (8 to 12 US fl oz). Highball cocktails and other mixed beverages are served in it.
